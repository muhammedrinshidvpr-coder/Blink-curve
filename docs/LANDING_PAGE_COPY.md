## SOLUTION SECTION

**Section label:** The Solution

**Headline:**
> Connected awareness. Before the bend.

**Body:**
> BlindSight creates an invisible shield at every dangerous curve. The moment two vehicles approach the same bend from opposite directions, both drivers receive an instant warning—with enough time to react, reduce speed, and pass safely.

**Flow Steps:**

1. **Detection** — Vehicle A approaches a known blind curve. BlindSight detects its presence.
2. **Signal** — BlindSight broadcasts an encrypted presence signal to the curve's detection zone.
3. **Warning** — Vehicle B receives an instant alert: "Vehicle approaching from the other side."
4. **Response** — Both drivers slow down. The curve is navigated safely.

**Tagline:**
> No infrastructure changes. No hardware required. Just awareness, exactly when you need it.

## FAQ SECTION

**Section label:** Questions

**Headline:**
> Everything you're wondering.

**Q1:** How does BlindSight know a vehicle is approaching?
> In the MVP phase, the app uses GPS position to detect when two vehicles are approaching the same known blind curve from opposite directions. In future phases, roadside devices provide hardware-grade detection.

**Q2:** Does it work without internet?
> The Phase 1 app requires cellular connectivity. Starting Phase 3, roadside nodes communicate via LPWAN (LoRa technology), enabling offline detection without cellular coverage.

**Q3:** Which roads are covered?
> BlindSight launches with pre-mapped data for Kerala's most dangerous curves: Vagamon, Munnar, Idukki, and Wayanad ghat roads. Coverage expands continuously.

**Q4:** How accurate are the alerts?
> Alert accuracy depends on GPS precision (3–5m in clear conditions) and the position database of known blind curves. We target <1% false positive rate and <0.1% miss rate.

**Q5:** Do both vehicles need the app?
> In Phase 1, yes—both vehicles need the app active. Phase 3 roadside nodes detect vehicles passively, so any vehicle is detected regardless of app status.

**Q6:** Is this legal to use while driving?
> BlindSight uses audio-first alerts designed to be received without looking at the screen. Identical to navigation app alerts already approved for driving use.
